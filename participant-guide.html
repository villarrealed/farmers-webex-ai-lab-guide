Webex AI Agent Lab

Farmers Insurance — Policy Servicing Use Case

Duration: ~52 minutes | Level: Hands-on | Focus: Webex AI Agent Studio

Welcome

Welcome to the Farmers Insurance Webex AI Agent Lab. In this hands-on session, you'll build an AI-powered virtual agent that helps policyholders add vehicles to their insurance policy — anytime, anywhere, without waiting for business hours.

By the end of this lab, you will have:

  • Created an autonomous AI agent in Webex AI Agent Studio
  • Built a knowledge base with insurance policy information
  • Connected pre-configured fulfillment actions via MCP servers
  • Tested your agent using built-in voice and chat previews

Prerequisites:

  • Access to Webex AI Agent Studio (credentials provided by your instructor)
  • A modern web browser (Chrome recommended)

Use Case: "After-Hours Vehicle Addition"

The Scenario: It's 9:47 PM on a Saturday night. Sarah Martinez just signed the paperwork on a 2024 Honda CR-V at a dealership. Her two kids are asleep in the back of her trade-in. The finance manager tells her: "We need proof of insurance covering this specific vehicle before we can hand you the keys."

Sarah calls Farmers Insurance and hears: "Our offices are currently closed. Business hours are Monday through Friday, 8 AM to 6 PM..."

The dealership closes in 28 minutes.

If Sarah can't get proof of insurance tonight, she has to:

  • Leave the new car at the dealership
  • Come back Monday (taking time off work)
  • Hope the financing terms don't require renegotiation after the weekend
  • Deal with two disappointed kids who were promised a ride home in the new car

Why This Matters to Farmers Insurance:

Business ImpactData Point
Vehicle purchases outside business hours47% occur evenings/weekends
Customer satisfaction drop from delayed service34% consider switching carriers
Average policy cancellation cost$1,200/year in lost premium
Competitor digital adoptionProgressive, GEICO, USAA offer 24/7 self-service

This isn't an edge case — it's nearly half of all vehicle purchases. Every one of these is a moment where Farmers either proves its value or pushes a policyholder toward a competitor.

The Solution We'll Build Today

An autonomous AI agent in Webex AI Agent Studio that:

  1. Answers immediately — no hold time, no business hours restriction
  2. Verifies Sarah's identity — policy number, last name, date of birth
  3. Collects the new vehicle details — year, make, model, VIN, primary use
  4. Confirms and processes the addition — adds the CR-V to her existing auto policy
  5. Asks how she'd like proof of insurance delivered:
    • SMS — a link texted to her phone (fastest for showing the dealership)
    • Email — full PDF insurance card sent to her email
    • Mobile App — push notification in the Farmers app

Sarah's experience changes from a dead-end phone tree to a 3-minute conversation that ends with her showing the finance manager proof of insurance on her phone and driving home.

Key Concepts:

  • Autonomous AI Agent — Uses large language models (LLMs) for natural, adaptive conversations. Understands varied phrasing without explicit programming for each variation.
  • Knowledge Base — Curated documents the agent references for accurate, grounded answers specific to your company.
  • MCP Server Actions — Pre-built integrations that let the agent take real action (verify identity, add vehicle, deliver documents) by calling backend systems.
  • AI Assistant — A separate Cisco AI feature that helps human agents when interactions escalate — providing transfer summaries and suggested responses.

Sarah might say "I just bought a car" or "I need to add a vehicle" or "Can you update my insurance?" — an autonomous agent powered by an LLM understands all these variations without programming each one. Scripted agents are better for highly structured flows where exact repeatability is critical.

Step 3: Write Agent Instructions

3
Write Agent Instructions

Navigate to the Profile tab. In the System Persona field, paste the following:

You are a friendly, professional insurance assistant for Farmers Insurance. Your name is Alex. You help policyholders manage their auto insurance policies, with a specialty in adding new vehicles to existing policies. Your tone is warm but efficient — you understand that customers contacting you often need help urgently (like getting proof of insurance at a car dealership). Be empathetic to their time pressure while ensuring you collect all necessary information accurately.

In the Agent Goal field, paste:

Help Farmers Insurance policyholders add new vehicles to their existing auto insurance policies. Verify the customer's identity, collect vehicle information, process the addition, and deliver updated proof of insurance via the customer's preferred method (SMS, email, or mobile app).

Now expand the Instructions section and paste:

## Conversation Flow 1. GREETING: Warmly greet the customer and ask how you can help today. 2. IDENTITY VERIFICATION: Before making any policy changes, verify the customer's identity by asking for: - Their policy number OR the phone number on file - Last name on the policy - Date of birth of the primary policyholder 3. COLLECT VEHICLE INFORMATION: Once verified, collect: - Year of the vehicle - Make (manufacturer) - Model - VIN (Vehicle Identification Number) — explain this is the 17-character code found on the dashboard or driver's side door jamb - Primary use (commute, pleasure, business) 4. DELIVERY PREFERENCE: Ask how they'd like their updated proof of insurance delivered: - SMS (text message to phone on file) - Email (PDF to email on file) - Mobile App (push notification in Farmers app) 5. CONFIRM AND PROCESS: Read back all collected information and ask for confirmation before processing. 6. COMPLETION: After processing, inform the customer that: - The vehicle has been added to their policy - Proof of insurance is being delivered via their chosen method - Provide a confirmation number - Ask if there's anything else you can help with ## Guardrails - Never provide specific premium amounts — inform the customer they will receive an updated billing statement - If the customer asks about coverage types or limits, provide general information from the knowledge base but recommend speaking with their agent for personalized advice - If you cannot verify the customer's identity after 2 attempts, offer to transfer to the next available agent - Always confirm information before processing any changes - Do not proceed with any policy changes until identity is verified

Click Save Changes.

Why such detailed instructions? These instructions are sent to the LLM with every conversation turn. They directly control the agent's behavior — when to ask for info, when to refuse, when to escalate. Without them, the agent "wings it" and may share inappropriate information or skip verification steps. Think of instructions as the agent's training manual that it reads before every single response.

Module 3: Configure Actions

Estimated time: 10 minutes

Actions let your agent DO things — not just talk about them.

Step 1: Connect Pre-Configured MCP Server Actions

1
Connect Pre-Configured MCP Server Actions

Your lab environment has three MCP server actions already provisioned. You'll connect them to your agent.

  1. In your agent configuration, navigate to the Actions tab
  2. Click "+ Add Action"
  3. Select "MCP Server" as the action source
  4. You should see the three pre-configured actions listed — select all three and click Connect
What are MCP Servers? Model Context Protocol (MCP) servers are standardized integrations that let your AI agent call external systems. Think of them as pre-built "skills" — someone has already built the connection to Farmers' backend systems (policy database, SMS gateway, email service). You just tell your agent when and how to use them.

Notice the pattern: Each action has clearly defined inputs. The AI agent's job is to collect this information naturally through conversation — not as a rigid form, but as a flowing dialogue. The agent decides when it has enough info to call each action.

Step 3: Understand How Actions Connect

3
Understand How Actions Connect
The agent decides when to call each action based on conversation context:

1. Customer says they want to add a vehicle → Agent begins verification
2. Agent has collected name + DOB + policy/phone → Calls verify_policyholder_identity
3. Verification succeeds → Agent collects vehicle info
4. Agent has all vehicle details + confirmation → Calls add_vehicle_to_policy
5. Addition succeeds → Agent asks delivery preference → Calls deliver_proof_of_insurance

You don't program this sequence — the LLM follows your instructions and triggers actions when the right information is available.

Click Save Changes after confirming all three actions are connected.

✓ Checkpoint: Your agent now has the ability to verify customers, add vehicles, and deliver proof of insurance — all through pre-configured MCP server integrations.
In production: Your engineering team builds and maintains MCP servers that connect to real policy management systems, SMS gateways, and email services. The conversational AI team (that's you) focuses on the customer experience — the instructions, knowledge, and testing — without needing to write backend code.
